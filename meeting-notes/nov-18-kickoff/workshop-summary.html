1. Homepage Vision

The team agreed the homepage should be transformed from an operations page to a marketing page:

  • Less text, more visuals
  • Scrolling images showing people having fun at events
  • Target audience: 25% of traffic from search (prospects) plus members
  • Big category "buckets" of activities (volunteering, learning about town, meeting friends, fun activities)
  • Committee highlights with photos - "picture and a blurb"
  • Convey key stats: "Nearly 1,000 members, 60-70 events a month"

2. Navigation Decisions

Public-facing (outside login):

  • Why join us / marketing homepage
  • Public event calendar
  • Proposed: "Newcomers Guide to Santa Barbara" (links to DMV, voter registration, volunteer orgs, cultural institutions)

Member-only (behind login):

  • Discussion of hamburger menu and current navigation
  • Website launched 2003, last updated 2014
  • Many redundant pages from old SEO practices
  • Search function: "Either fix it or kill it"

3. Brand Voice Gap (Major Discussion Point)

Instagram Brand

  • Curated, elegant, minimal
  • Professional photography
  • Aspirational lifestyle
  • Polished graphics

Website Brand

  • Dense layouts, lots of information
  • Exclamation points throughout
  • 645 pages of content
  • "Not very inviting"
  • Website feels "dated" and "busy"

Key insight: "Members experience our brand differently depending upon where they encounter us"

Question raised: Is this gap intentional or problematic?

4. Three Task Forces Proposed

1

Navigation & Pages

  • Define overall navigation structure
  • Determine what each page should do
  • Can be descriptive: "What job is this page designed to do?"
2

Brand Voice

  • Distill the brand voice
  • Goal: Create brand book/guidelines
  • Document artifacts and key messages
3

Event Discovery

  • Highest user engagement area
  • How do members find/sign up for events?
  • Member interface to events

5. Budget & Resources

$5,000 budgeted for this project (set aside at beginning of term)

Discussion of hiring a designer for a "modest fee" to focus on:

  • Turning prototypes into presentable mockups
  • Branding guide
  • Navigation mockups

"Don't need coders - have people who can implement"

6. Technical Requirements

  • Mobile responsive is critical - 1/3 of users on mobile
  • Mobile website should replace/be the app experience
  • Design websites that "automatically work on mobile"

7. Prototyping Approach

  • Sketches preferred over polished prototypes initially
  • "Don't make it look like you put your heart and soul into it" - keeps feedback honest
  • Can use AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini) to create mockups
  • Prototypes don't need depth/detail - "communicate a concept"

8. Next Steps & Timeline

  • Board meeting presentation target
  • Thanksgiving timing consideration
  • Next meeting: Present prototypes, get group feedback, make decisions
  • Goal: Develop concepts to show at board meeting

9. Additional Ideas Mentioned

  • AI-generated jingles from 11 Labs (country style, Madison Ave, political ad) - demo planned
  • Volunteer organization database (vetted by Volunteers in Action committee) - currently hidden, needs visibility
  • Proposal for "Newcomers Guide to Santa Barbara" as public resource
