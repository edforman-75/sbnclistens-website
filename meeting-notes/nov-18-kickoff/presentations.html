Workshop Presentations

Four comprehensive presentations delivered at the November 18 workshop, covering research findings, analytics, priority pages, and the workshop plan.

Overview

These presentations formed the foundation of the November 18 workshop, providing team members with research insights, data analysis, and a structured plan for collaborative problem-solving. Each presentation builds on the others to create a complete picture of user needs and opportunities for improvement.

Presentation 1: Research Findings (Qualitative Analysis)

ðŸ’¬

SBNC Research Presentation

Duration: Approximately 20-30 minutes
Focus: User interview insights and qualitative analysis

What's Covered:

  • 67 Pain Points identified across all user interviews
  • 134 Opportunities for website improvement
  • Key Themes: Navigation complexity, information overload, mobile challenges, search difficulties
  • User Quotes: Direct feedback from SBNC members about their website experience
  • Priority Areas: Most critical issues affecting member satisfaction

Key Insights:

  • Navigation is the #1 challenge - too many menu items overwhelm users
  • New members struggle to find basic information (how to join events, guest policies, etc.)
  • Mobile experience needs significant improvement - members frequently use phones to check events
  • Search functionality exists but is hard to find and underutilized
  • Event calendar is popular and working well, but could be enhanced with better filtering

Presentation 2: Website Analytics (Quantitative Analysis)

ðŸ“ˆ

SBNC Website Analytics

Duration: Approximately 15-20 minutes
Focus: Traffic patterns, user behavior, and engagement metrics

What's Covered:

  • 554 Users during analysis period (Nov 6-13, 2025)
  • 6,627 Pageviews showing high engagement
  • 18,463 Total Interactions with site elements
  • Most Popular Pages: Event calendar dominates traffic
  • User Flow Analysis: How members navigate through the site
  • Device Breakdown: Desktop vs. mobile usage patterns

Key Findings:

  • Event calendar is by far the most visited section - members return frequently to check events
  • High return visitor rate indicates members are engaged and checking regularly
  • Mobile traffic is significant - responsive design is critical
  • Members spend most time on event pages and member directory
  • Some key pages (like new member resources) get surprisingly low traffic - discoverability issue

How the Presentations Connect

1. Identify Problems

Research Presentation reveals what members struggle with through their own words and experiences.

2. Validate with Data

Website Analytics confirms patterns with traffic data and shows which pages need attention.

3. Prioritize Solutions

Priority Pages focuses the team on 5 specific areas where improvements will have the biggest impact.

4. Plan Action

Workshop Agenda structures collaborative work to turn insights into concrete improvements.

Using These Presentations

These presentations can be reused for:

  • Board Reports: Share research findings and progress with SBNC leadership
  • New Team Member Onboarding: Help new volunteers understand the project context
  • Task Force Kickoffs: Reference specific findings relevant to each group's work
  • Progress Updates: Show before/after comparisons as improvements are implemented
  • Future Workshops: Template for similar user research initiatives
