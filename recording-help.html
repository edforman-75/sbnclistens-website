How to Record Your Interview

Simple, step-by-step instructions for every platform. Choose what works for you!

üéØ Quick Recommendations

üì± In-Person Interview?

Use your phone's voice memo app

iPhone Voice Memos or Android Voice Recorder - both are free, built-in, and super easy!

üíª Remote Interview?

Use Zoom recording

Free Zoom account works perfectly. Recording saves automatically when meeting ends.

üì± Platform-Specific Instructions

üì± iPhone Voice Memos

  1. Open Voice Memos app (red icon with waveform)
  2. Tap the red record button
  3. Place phone on table between you and interviewee (6-12 inches from speakers)
  4. Tap red square to stop when interview is complete
  5. Share ‚Üí Save to Files or email to yourself

Tips:

  • Recording continues even if screen locks
  • Files save as .m4a (perfect for upload!)
  • Keep phone plugged in for longer recordings

üìñ Detailed Apple Guide ‚Üí

üì± Android Voice Recorder

Samsung/Google Pixel:

  1. Open "Voice Recorder" or "Recorder" app
  2. Tap red microphone button
  3. Place phone on table between speakers
  4. Tap stop button when finished
  5. Share ‚Üí Upload to Google Drive or email

Other Android phones: Download "Easy Voice Recorder" (free from Play Store)

Tips:

  • Make sure not in airplane mode
  • Disable battery saver during recording
  • Check storage space before starting

üìñ Android Recording Help ‚Üí

üíª Zoom Recording (Remote)

  1. Start a Zoom meeting (free account works!)
  2. Send join link to your interviewee
  3. Click "Record" button at bottom of screen
  4. Select "Record to this Computer"
  5. Recording auto-saves when you end the meeting

Where Zoom saves files:

  • Mac: /Users/[YourName]/Documents/Zoom
  • PC: C:\Users\[YourName]\Documents\Zoom

üìñ Zoom Recording Guide ‚Üí

üíª Mac QuickTime

  1. Open QuickTime Player (in Applications)
  2. File ‚Üí New Audio Recording
  3. Click the red record button
  4. Click stop button when interview is done
  5. File ‚Üí Save (choose location and name)

Tips:

  • Use built-in microphone or external mic
  • Place laptop between you and interviewee
  • Test 10 seconds first to check levels

üíª Windows Voice Recorder

  1. Click Start menu ‚Üí Type "Voice Recorder"
  2. Open Voice Recorder app
  3. Click the microphone button to start recording
  4. Click stop button when interview is complete
  5. Right-click on recording ‚Üí "Open file location" to find file

Default save location:

C:\Users\[YourName]\Documents\Sound recordings

üé§ Getting Consent to Record

Always Ask Permission First!

Use this script before starting the recording:

"I'd like to record our conversation so we can transcribe and review it accurately. The recording will only be used by our team to analyze themes. Your name won't be attached to quotes. And if you'd prefer complete anonymity, just let me know. Is it okay if I record?"

Important: Wait for a clear verbal "Yes" before starting the recording!

üí° Quick Tips for Good Recordings

‚úÖ Test First

Do a 30-second test recording to make sure everything works

üìè Placement

Place device 6-12 inches from speakers for best audio quality

üîá Silent Mode

Put phone on silent (recording still works!) to avoid interruptions

üíæ Check Storage

Need 15-30 MB free space for a 15-minute recording

üîå Power Up

Plug in your device if possible for longer recordings

üëÇ Verify

Listen to first 10 seconds after recording to make sure it worked

üì§ After You Record

  1. STOP the recording - Make sure recording has ended and saved
  2. LISTEN to first 10 seconds - Verify you can hear both voices clearly
  3. NAME the file - Use format: SBNC_Interview_[Name]_Nov[Date] (example: SBNC_Interview_Jane_Nov12)
  4. SUBMIT via online form - Upload recording and answer 3 quick questions
