Tips for Using This Template

Personalize It!

  • Add a personal greeting if you know them well
  • Mention how you know them or why you thought of them
  • Adjust the tone to match your relationship
  • Feel free to shorten it if emailing someone you know well

Example Variations

For someone you know well:

Hi Sarah,

Hope you're doing well! Quick ask - I'm helping improve the SBNC website and would love your input. Do you have 15 minutes sometime this week for a quick chat about your experience using it? Can be phone or coffee - your choice!

No prep needed, just your honest thoughts. Let me know if you're free!

Thanks,
[Your name]

For a former member:

Hi Tom,

I'm part of a project to improve the SBNC website, and I'd really value your perspective as someone who was previously involved with the club.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick conversation about your experience with the website when you were a member? Your honest feedback would help us make it work better for everyone.

No pressure at all - just let me know if you're interested!

Best,
[Your name]

For someone less engaged:

Hi Jennifer,

I noticed you joined SBNC a while back, and I'm curious about your experience! I'm helping with a project to improve the website, and I'd love to hear your perspective.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick chat? Whether you use the site a lot or rarely, your honest feedback would be really valuable.

Let me know if you're available this week or next!

Thanks,
[Your name]
