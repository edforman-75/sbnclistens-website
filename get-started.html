Get Started with Pre-Work Interviews

Ready to help improve the SBNC website? Great! Here's your simple path forward.

Remember: This is completely optional but incredibly valuable. Even doing just one interview helps!

Your Timeline: 5 Simple Steps

  1. Read the Guides (30 minutes)

    Download and read the interview guide and cheat sheet. They teach you everything you need to know about conducting great conversations.

    Download Guides
  2. Check Who's Already Being Interviewed (IMPORTANT!)

    Before finding people to interview, check our tracking list to see who's already been claimed by other interviewers. This prevents us from contacting the same people multiple times.

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Check First, Then Register

    Step 1: Check the tracking spreadsheet to see if someone is already listed

    üìä View Tracking List

    Step 2: Once you've identified people who AREN'T on the list, register them so others know you're interviewing them

    ‚úèÔ∏è Register Your Interviewees

    Why this matters: We want to spread our interviews across as many different members as possible to get diverse perspectives!

  3. Find 2-3 People to Interview

    Think about members who've used the SBNC website. Variety is key! Look for recent joiners, inactive members, or former members - not just your super-engaged friends.

    üéØ Who to Seek Out

    • Recent members (joined in last 6-12 months)
    • Inactive current members (why aren't they engaged?)
    • People who attended once and didn't return
    • Former members who let membership lapse
    • People who considered joining but didn't

    Why? Highly engaged members figured out workarounds. People who struggled tell us what needs fixing!

  4. Conduct Your Interviews (15 minutes each)

    Have friendly conversations! Press record, ask your questions, let them talk, and be genuinely curious. That's it!

    Recording Help
  5. Submit via Online Form (3 minutes per interview)

    Upload your recording, answer 3 quick questions, and you're done! We handle all the analysis.

    Submit Interview

Quick Tips for Success

üëÇ Listen 80%

Let them do most of the talking. Your job is to be curious and ask "why?"

ü§î Be Curious

You're not interrogating - you're a curious friend trying to understand their experience.

üéâ It's Actually Fun!

People genuinely enjoy sharing their experiences. You'll be surprised by the great conversations.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Seek Diverse Perspectives

Don't just interview super-engaged members! The most valuable insights come from people who joined but aren't very active, people who attended once and didn't return, or former members who let membership lapse. Highly engaged members figured out workarounds - they won't tell us what's broken!
