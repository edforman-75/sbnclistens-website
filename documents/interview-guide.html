Workshop Pre-Work: Your Guide to Great Conversations

Welcome! You're helping prepare for the November 18th workshop where we'll tackle SBNC website issues.

The goal: Fix 7-8 critical pages before end of term.

Your role: Have 2-3 casual 15-minute conversations with people who've used the website.

Who Makes a Good Interviewee?

Anyone who's interacted with the SBNC website! More variety = better:

  • Someone considering joining
  • Recent members (joined last 6-12 months)
  • Active current members
  • Inactive current members (VERY VALUABLE!)
  • Former members
  • Mix of ages

CRITICAL: Seek Beyond the Super-Engaged!

  • ‚úÖ People who joined but aren't very active - Why not?
  • ‚úÖ People who attended once and didn't return - What happened?
  • ‚úÖ People who browse website but rarely attend - What's the barrier?
  • ‚úÖ Former members who let membership lapse - Why?
  • ‚úÖ People who considered joining but didn't - What stopped them?

Why? Highly engaged members figured out workarounds. People who struggled tell us what needs fixing!

Why Interviews Instead of a Survey?

What Surveys Tell Us:

  • Surface-level opinions ("The website is confusing")
  • What people can articulate in writing
  • Answers to questions WE think are important

What Interviews Reveal:

  • What people actually do
  • The "why" behind frustrations
  • Things they don't realize are important
  • Emotional reactions
  • Stories that reveal patterns
  • Ability to dig deeper: "Tell me more..."

Real Example:

Survey: "The events calendar is hard to use."

Interview: "Well, I was trying to find tennis events... I clicked on 'Activities' but that showed everything. Then I tried the calendar but I couldn't filter by sport. So I gave up and just looked at the monthly newsletter instead."

See the difference? The interview reveals exactly what to fix!

The 3 Big Ideas Behind Great Interviews

1. LISTEN WAY MORE THAN YOU TALK

Aim for 80/20: they talk 80%, you talk 20%.

2. BE GENUINELY CURIOUS

You're not interrogating. You're a curious friend trying to understand.

3. MAKE IT SAFE TO BE HONEST

People worry about hurting feelings. Encourage honesty!

The Interview Structure (15 minutes)

INTRO (1 minute)

"Thank you for speaking with me. I'm researching the SBNC website experience. No right/wrong answers. Your honest feedback helps us improve.

I'd like to record this conversation so we can transcribe and review it accurately. The recording will only be used by our team. Your name won't be attached to quotes. And if you'd prefer complete anonymity, just let me know. Is it okay if I record?

[Get clear YES, then START RECORDING]

15 minutes total. Questions before we start?"

WARM-UP (2 minutes)

Grounding questions first:

  • How long in Santa Barbara area?
  • When did you join/hear about SBNC?
  • What attracted you to the club?
  • How would you describe SBNC to someone new to SB?

For recent members (joined last 6-12 months):

  • How did you first find out about SBNC?
  • Walk me through trying to join - what did you do?
  • What was that experience like?
  • Was anything confusing when trying to join?

Then: How did you first hear about/visit the website?

EXPLORATION (10 minutes)

Ask open-ended questions like:

  • "Tell me about the last time you used the website..."
  • "Walk me through what you were trying to do..."
  • "What was that experience like?"
  • "What went through your mind when...?"

Follow up with:

  • "Why?"
  • "Tell me more about that..."
  • "What do you mean by...?"
  • "Can you give me an example?"

SPECIFIC PAGES (2 minutes)

Ask about pages they mentioned or key pages like:

  • Homepage
  • Events calendar
  • Membership info
  • Activity groups

CLOSING (1 minute)

"Is there anything else about the website that frustrates you or that you wish worked better?"

"Thank you so much. This is really helpful!"

[STOP RECORDING]

Question Bank (Use as needed - don't ask all!)

General Website Experience:

  • What was your first impression of the website?
  • What do you typically use the website for?
  • What's the most frustrating thing about the site?

Navigation:

  • How do you usually find what you're looking for?
  • Have you ever gotten lost on the site?
  • Is there anything you've looked for but couldn't find?

Specific Features:

  • How do you stay informed about club events?
  • How do you find activities you're interested in?
  • Have you tried to RSVP or register for something?

For Recent Members:

  • How did you decide to join SBNC?
  • What information were you looking for when considering joining?
  • Was it easy to figure out how to become a member?

Recording Tips

Before:

  • Test your recording device
  • Place 6-12 inches from speakers
  • Check storage space

During:

  • Put phone on silent (recording still works!)
  • Check recording still going at 5-min mark
  • If it fails, start new recording and continue

After:

  • Stop recording immediately
  • Listen to first 10 seconds to verify
  • Name file: SBNC_Interview_[Name]_[Date]

After the Interview

Submit via the online form:

  1. Upload recording
  2. Answer 3 quick context questions
  3. Submit

Takes 3 minutes!
