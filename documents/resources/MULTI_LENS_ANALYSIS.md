## Complete Multi-Lens Pattern Analysis

*Patterns identified across 5 interviews through 12 analytical lenses*

## LENS 1: Pain Points - Problem Clusters

*What frustrates users and blocks their goals*

### Process Friction (9 issues - EFFORT category)
**Pattern:** Users struggle with complex, multi-step processes for basic tasks

- Difficulty in contacting committee chairs through the app
- The process for canceling events is clunky and not automated
- Difficulties in canceling event registrations
- Confusing event cancellation process
- Inability to easily find signed-up events on the website
- Slow payment process on the website due to additional security measures
- Inaccessibility of board member descriptions
- Difficulty navigating to important pages on the website

**Representative Quote:** *"There should just be a way to automate that somehow...."*

**Impact:** Creates unnecessary effort and potential for miscommunication, leading to user frustration and workarounds

### Navigation Challenges (6 issues - TIME category)
**Pattern:** Website requires excessive backtracking and lacks intuitive flow

- Difficulty navigating the website compared to the app, requiring excessive backtracking
- Overwhelming and cluttered website navigation
- Confusing navigation and overwhelming amount of information on the website and app
- Absence of a search functionality on the website for events
- Lack of a filter or sorting option for events
- Lack of visibility of event registration status on the website

**Representative Quote:** *"I feel like I'm, I have to go back a lot more steps that way than with the app...."*

**Impact:** Increases time spent on tasks, leading to frustration and abandonment

### Information Quality (5 issues - QUALITY category)
**Pattern:** Information is unclear, incomplete, or inaccurate

- Lack of clarity on event registration status (full or open) due to incorrect information display
- Unclear terms and conditions and document readability during registration
- Unclear committee descriptions leading to confusion
- No archive for newsletters on the website
- Inconsistency in information delivery between the app and email notifications

**Representative Quote:** *"Sometimes, I think it's a little frustrating it'll be like a hold the date or that it'll be. Oh, it's registration full..."*

**Impact:** Leads to confusion and missed opportunities

### Social Connection (3 issues - SOCIAL category)
**Pattern:** Barriers to connecting with other members

- Lack of visibility for alumni in the directory
- Need for alumni visibility in the directory
- Lack of awareness about specific member committees

**Representative Quote:** *"How do they find each other after they're done?..."*

**Impact:** Limits networking opportunities

### Emotional Friction (2 issues - EMOTIONAL category)
**Pattern:** Users experience anxiety and disengagement

- Inability to easily see which events are full or have waitlists
- Unclear terms and conditions and document readability during registration

**Representative Quote:** *"I think it would be helpful to know which events still have openings or have it blocked out..."*

**Impact:** Leads to frustration and disengagement from the platform

## LENS 2: Jobs to be Done - User Goals

*What users are trying to accomplish*

### Functional Jobs (10 identified)

**DISCOVERY JOBS** (Most frequent)

1. When I am trying to find events on the website, I want to easily navigate and search for events by category or keyword, so I can efficiently find what I am interested in.
   - Current Pain: No search functionality, must scroll through calendar
   - Desired Outcome: Search bar or category filters

2. When I want to see what events I have signed up for, I want to find this information easily, so I can keep track of my commitments.
   - Current Pain: Unaware of how to find this on website, relies on email
   - Desired Outcome: Easy way to view signed-up events

3. When I want to find events to attend, I want to easily navigate the website or app, so I can plan my participation.
   - Desired Outcome: Organized view of relevant events

**REGISTRATION JOBS**

4. When I want to sign up for an event, I want a straightforward process, so I can confirm my participation easily.
   - Current Pain: Additional security checks slow down process
   - Desired Outcome: Streamlined sign-up process

5. When I want to sign up for events, I want to easily navigate the website or app, so I can register without frustration.
   - Desired Outcome: Straightforward and quick registration

**COMMUNITY JOBS**

6. When I want to contact committee members, I want to easily access the member directory, so I can connect with them.
   - Desired Outcome: Easy and user-friendly directory access

7. When I want to join a social club, I want to sign up easily, so I can become a member and engage with the community.
   - Desired Outcome: Straightforward registration process

**MANAGEMENT JOBS**

8. When I cannot attend an event, I want to inform the committee chair quickly, so I can keep them updated and allow others to attend.
   - Desired Outcome: Simple method to cancel or notify about attendance changes

9. When I need to find information about committee roles, I want clear descriptions and access to relevant documents, so I can understand my responsibilities.
   - Desired Outcome: Clear and organized committee descriptions

10. When I am looking for events to participate in, I want to easily find and sign up for them, so I can stay engaged and enjoy the activities.
    - Desired Outcome: Smoother navigation that remembers position on calendar

### Emotional Jobs (7 identified)

1. When I am navigating the website, I want to feel confident that I can find what I need, so I can enjoy my experience without frustration.

2. When I sign up for events, I want to feel valued and acknowledged, so I can enjoy being part of the community.

3. When I am trying to navigate the website, I want to feel confident in my ability to find information, so I can engage without anxiety.

4. When I participate in events, I want to feel connected to others, so I can foster relationships within the community.

5. When I visit the website, I want to feel invited and engaged, so I can enjoy using it and feel part of the community.

6. When I join a new community, I want to feel welcomed and integrated, so I can enjoy a sense of belonging.

7. When I use the app or website, I want to feel confident that I can find what I need, so I can enjoy my experience without frustration.

**About Information Quality:**

**About Search & Discovery:**
- "I think it would be easier if you could pick whatever committee that you think"
- "No, I actually haven't. Yeah. I look at my emails to see what I've signed up for"

### THINKS (Mental models & expectations)

**Expectations of Self-Service:**
- Users expect to manage their own registrations independently
- Believe cancellation should be automated like other modern platforms
- Assume search functionality should be standard on websites

**Utility-First Mindset:**
- "I want to do my business and I want to get out" - prioritize functionality over aesthetics
- "Cleaner, simpler, better is my approach" - prefer minimalist design

**Comparison to App:**
- Website should have feature parity with mobile app
- App sets expectations for what website should offer
- Inconsistency creates confusion about platform capabilities

### DOES (Behavioral patterns)

**Workarounds:**
- Checks email instead of website to see registered events
- Contacts committee chairs directly for cancellations
- Uses app instead of website when possible
- Scrolls through entire calendar due to lack of search

**Avoidance:**
- Actively avoids website in favor of app
- Skips reading terms and conditions due to poor readability
- Doesn't use certain features because they're hard to find

**Reliance on External Systems:**
- Depends on email for event tracking
- Uses personal communication channels instead of platform features

### FEELS (Emotional responses)

**Frustration:**
- When excessive backtracking is required
- When information is unclear or inaccurate
- When simple tasks require complex processes

**Anxiety:**
- About whether registration went through
- About missing events due to poor information
- About understanding commitments and responsibilities

**Disconnection:**
- When can't easily connect with alumni or other members
- When platform doesn't feel inviting

**Efficiency Pressure:**
- Want to complete tasks quickly
- Feel time is wasted on navigation

## LENS 5: POV Statements - Problem Framing

*Point of View statements that frame user needs*

### High Priority POV Statements

**POV #1: Mobile App Reliability**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need an improved mobile app experience because they often find it confusing and unreliable, leading to frustration when trying to register for events.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "The phone app says you registered. But you know what? I'm looking at the actual log here. And your registration expired because you didn't pay within the first 15 minutes."
- **Underlying Need:** A seamless and reliable mobile experience for event registration
- **Key Insight:** Users primarily rely on their phones, and inconsistencies create barriers to participation
- **Priority:** HIGH

**POV #2: Committee Training**
- **Statement:** Committee chairs need better training on the event management system because they struggle with the complexity of the process, hindering their ability to execute successful events.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "The training process that is undertaken in good faith is really not adequate to allow a committee co-chair to be facile in terms of how to understand and how to use to its greatest potential."
- **Underlying Need:** Effective training resources for new users of the event management system
- **Key Insight:** Without proper training, new committee members feel overwhelmed and underprepared, affecting event quality
- **Priority:** HIGH

**POV #3: Technical Support**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need a dedicated technical support person because the administrative tasks can overwhelm volunteers, preventing them from focusing on event planning.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "Having that kind of capacity would be significant...the mechanics of what I'm going to refer to as the back office work."
- **Underlying Need:** Technical assistance to alleviate administrative burdens
- **Key Insight:** Reducing administrative strain allows volunteers to apply their efforts toward creative event planning
- **Priority:** HIGH

### Medium Priority POV Statements

**POV #4: Event Information Clarity**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need clearer event descriptions and scheduling information because they often encounter confusion over conflicting details, impacting their ability to plan effectively.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "Sometimes the event descriptions and especially save the date kinds of things or conflicting scheduling things were not as transparent or not as clear as they could be."
- **Underlying Need:** Accurate and accessible information about events
- **Key Insight:** Clarity in communication is essential for engagement and attendance at events
- **Priority:** MEDIUM

**POV #5: Institutional Knowledge**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need a way to document and share institutional knowledge because the frequent turnover of committee members creates a lack of continuity and memory for best practices.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "The lack of a formalized institutional memory...to be able to pass that baton to the next term."
- **Underlying Need:** A reliable system for capturing and sharing historical knowledge
- **Key Insight:** Continuity in processes is vital for organizational success and member satisfaction
- **Priority:** MEDIUM

**POV #6: Name Tag System**
- **Statement:** Event organizers need a streamlined system for generating printed name tags because it helps in identifying attendees and managing registrations effectively.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "I like the printed name tags as an audit function...to quickly spot...anyone walking around with a name tag that was a pencil, sharpie, handwritten name tag rather than a printed name tag."
- **Underlying Need:** An efficient method for managing event registrations and participant identification
- **Key Insight:** Clear identification enhances networking and ensures proper registration management
- **Priority:** MEDIUM

**POV #7: Registration Data Access**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need user-friendly access to event registration data because it allows them to manage their events more effectively and ensure participant satisfaction.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "The notion of doing printed name tags...is an issue when you've got over 100 people."
- **Underlying Need:** Easy access to registration and participant data
- **Key Insight:** Effective data management is critical for large event coordination and attendee experience
- **Priority:** MEDIUM

### Low Priority POV Statements

**POV #8: Website Vibrancy**
- **Statement:** Newcomers need a more vibrant and engaging online presence because a refreshed website can attract more participation from users who are seeking social connections.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "The good news and the bad news about newcomers is it reinvents itself every six months."
- **Underlying Need:** A dynamic and appealing platform for community engagement
- **Key Insight:** A vibrant online presence fosters community involvement and participation
- **Priority:** LOW

## LENS 6: Key Insights - Synthesized Learnings

*Critical patterns and discoveries from research*

### User Needs (3 insights - HIGH confidence)

**INSIGHT #1: Admin vs Regular User Disparity**
- **Statement:** Users with administrative privileges have a different experience than regular users, leading to insights about potential usability issues.
- **Evidence:** "I had more problems with the phone app than I did with the computer based web application."
- **Why It Matters:** Understanding the disparity in experiences can inform design decisions to cater to both administrative and regular users, ensuring that functionalities meet the needs of all user types.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

**INSIGHT #2: Clarity and Transparency Expectations**
- **Statement:** Users value clarity and transparency in event information but find descriptions often lacking.
- **Evidence:** "Event descriptions and especially save the date kinds of things or conflicting scheduling things were not as transparent or not as clear..."
- **Why It Matters:** Enhancing the clarity of event information could improve user engagement and event participation, as users are more likely to attend events they clearly understand.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

**INSIGHT #3: Demand for Technical Support**
- **Statement:** There is a demand for a dedicated technical support role to assist with event management.
- **Evidence:** "There ought to be a paid technical kind of person that really could be there, you know, as the glue to hold together..."
- **Why It Matters:** Implementing a dedicated support role could relieve administrative burdens from committee members and improve event execution.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

### Behavioral Patterns (2 insights - HIGH/MEDIUM confidence)

**INSIGHT #4: Mobile App Underpowered**
- **Statement:** The mobile app is perceived as underpowered and creates confusion among users.
- **Evidence:** "I felt it was underpowered and gave conflicting information."
- **Why It Matters:** Improving the mobile app's functionality could enhance user satisfaction and reduce frustration, particularly among users who primarily rely on mobile devices.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

**INSIGHT #5: Printed Name Tags as Audit Function**
- **Statement:** Printed name tags serve a dual purpose of identification and as an audit function for event registration.
- **Evidence:** "If I saw anyone walking around with a name tag that was a pencil, sharpie, handwritten name tag rather than a printed name tag... that was a clue that that person may not have been registered..."
- **Why It Matters:** Incorporating printed name tags can enhance event organization and registration verification, contributing to a smoother attendee experience.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

### Context & Mental Models (4 insights - HIGH confidence)

**INSIGHT #6: Training Gap**
- **Statement:** There is a significant knowledge gap in the training and onboarding process for new committee members.
- **Evidence:** "The training process really needs to have some hard, hard look at two issues... the venue for training has been woefully inadequate."
- **Why It Matters:** Addressing this gap can enable new members to effectively use the system, thereby improving overall event management and user experience.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

**INSIGHT #7: Lack of Institutional Memory**
- **Statement:** Institutional memory is lacking, leading to repeated learning curves for new members.
- **Evidence:** "The lack of a formalized institutional memory... there's not a facile way to have some kind of archival process..."
- **Why It Matters:** Creating a system for preserving institutional knowledge can smooth transitions and maintain continuity, benefiting both new and returning members.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

**INSIGHT #8: Utility Over Aesthetics**
- **Statement:** Users prioritize utility over aesthetics in website design.
- **Evidence:** "I want to do my business and I want to get out. And so from that perspective, cleaner, simpler, better is my approach."
- **Why It Matters:** Focusing on utility in design can lead to increased user satisfaction and more efficient interactions with the website.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

**INSIGHT #9: High Turnover Impact**
- **Statement:** The organization experiences high turnover, affecting continuity and event planning.
- **Evidence:** "It reinvents itself every six months... the learning curve just hits the peak at the time of transition."
- **Why It Matters:** A strategy to address turnover, such as extending terms or improving training, could enhance the stability and effectiveness of event planning.
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## LENS 12: Cross-Lens Synthesis

*Themes appearing across multiple analytical lenses*

### THEME 1: Self-Service Gap

**Appears across 7 lenses:**
- **Pain Point:** Can't find registered events, can't cancel events (LENS 1)
- **Job to be Done:** Want to manage own participation independently (LENS 2)
- **Does:** Checks email instead, contacts chairs directly (LENS 3)
- **POV Statement:** Need user-friendly access to registration data (LENS 5)
- **Insight:** Users expect self-service like modern platforms (LENS 6)
- **Journey Stage:** Frustration during Planning stage (LENS 8)
- **Opportunity:** How might we enable self-service event management? (LENS 4, 11)

**Design Implication:** Users expect modern self-service capabilities. The platform should allow users to view their registrations, cancel attendance, and manage their participation without needing to contact anyone.
