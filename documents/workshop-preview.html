Workshop Preview: What to Expect

November 18th Design Thinking Workshop

Overview

The SBNC Listens workshop is where we transform member interview insights into concrete website improvements. This is a collaborative, hands-on session focused on designing solutions together.

When & Where

Date: November 18th

Time: 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM (tentative)

Location: Ed & Jan's Home (address sent to registered participants)

Duration: Approximately 3 hours with breaks

What to Bring

  • Yourself! That's the most important thing
  • Your curiosity and willingness to collaborate
  • Laptop (optional) if you prefer digital sketching
  • Notes from interviews if you conducted pre-work

We provide:

  • All materials (paper, markers, whiteboards)
  • Interview findings presentation
  • Reference materials
  • Refreshments and snacks

Workshop Flow

Phase 1: The Reveal (6:00-6:30 PM)

What happens: We present the patterns and insights from all pre-work interviews

You'll see:

  • Real user quotes and stories
  • Pain points and frustrations
  • What members are trying to accomplish
  • Where the website is failing them

Your role: Listen, absorb, ask questions

Phase 2: Prioritize (6:30-7:00 PM)

What happens: As a group, we decide which 7-8 pages need fixing most urgently

You'll participate in:

  • Voting on priorities
  • Discussion about impact vs. effort
  • Reality check on timeline (before term ends!)

Your role: Share your perspective on what matters most

Phase 3: Design Solutions (7:00-8:30 PM)

What happens: Break into small groups, each tackling 2-3 pages

You'll do:

  • Brainstorm solutions to the problems we identified
  • Sketch wireframes or flows (rough is fine!)
  • Think creatively about improvements
  • Consider what users actually need

Your role: Active participation - drawing, discussing, ideating

This is the fun part! No coding required - just ideas and creativity.

Phase 4: Share & Plan (8:30-9:00 PM)

What happens: Groups present their solutions; we agree on the plan

You'll see:

  • Each group's proposed solutions
  • Discussion and refinement
  • Assignment of implementation tasks
  • Timeline to launch before term ends

Your role: Provide feedback, help make decisions

What Makes This "Abbreviated Design Thinking"?

Traditional Design Thinking takes weeks or months. We're compressing it to fit our timeline:

What we're keeping:

  • Empathy (interviews with real users)
  • Define (identifying the real problems)
  • Ideate (brainstorming solutions)
  • Prototype (sketching improvements)
  • Plan (implementation roadmap)

What we're streamlining:

  • Faster iteration cycles
  • Focus on 7-8 pages (not everything)
  • Quick decision-making
  • Parallel work streams

Workshop Atmosphere

  • Collaborative: Everyone's input matters, regardless of technical skill
  • Creative: Wild ideas welcome, criticism is constructive
  • Focused: Clear goals and limited time keep us on track
  • Casual: Comfortable home environment, snacks available
  • Productive: We'll leave with concrete decisions and action items

If You Did Pre-Work Interviews

Great! You'll have context when we present findings. You might recognize stories or quotes from your own interviews.

Don't stress about: Presenting your findings formally - we've already analyzed everything and will present the synthesis.

If You Didn't Do Pre-Work

No problem! We'll catch you up on everything at the start. Your fresh perspective on solutions is valuable!

Don't worry about: Being "behind" - everyone participates equally in the design phase.

Skills Needed

Required:

  • Use the SBNC website (even occasionally)
  • Care about improving member experience
  • Willingness to collaborate

NOT required:

  • Technical/coding skills
  • Web design expertise
  • Public speaking ability
  • Artistic talent

Preparation Tips

  1. Review the SBNC website - Browse a few pages, notice what works and what doesn't
  2. Think about your own experiences - What frustrated you? What was confusing?
  3. Come with an open mind - Ready to hear others' perspectives
  4. Don't over-prepare - Fresh perspectives are valuable!

After the Workshop

You'll walk away with:

  • Clear understanding of what's being fixed
  • Shared ownership of the solutions
  • Implementation timeline and assignments
  • Confidence that we're fixing what matters

The tech team handles:

  • All coding and implementation
  • Testing and quality assurance
  • Deployment before term ends

Questions?

Email: technology@sbnewcomers.org

We're excited to work with you on improving the SBNC website!
