1. Purpose of This Page

This showcase is written for designers and SBNC volunteers who want a quick, honest view of what Wild Apricot (WA) themes can and cannot do. It complements the Customization Techniques and Platform Limitations pages by focusing on the practical choice of theme family.

Key ideas:

  • Every SBNC page will sit inside a Wild Apricot theme.
  • Theme choice affects typography, spacing, header layout, and mobile behavior.
  • Even the “best” theme still inherits platform-wide limitations described elsewhere.

2. How Wild Apricot Themes Work

  • Each site uses one active theme at a time.
  • The theme defines:
    • Global header and footer layout.
    • Navigation placement and behavior.
    • Default fonts, colors, buttons, and form styles.
    • Standard page templates for system pages (login, profile, events, etc.).
  • Theme versions are updated by WA; major updates can change spacing or details but keep the overall structure.
  • Custom CSS can override some visual aspects but cannot fundamentally change the layout model.

3. Modern Responsive Themes (High-Level Overview)

Names and details may change over time, but modern WA themes generally fall into a few patterns. Examples below are meant as a guide for SBNC’s design discussions, not an exhaustive catalog.

3.1 Hero-Style Themes (Large banner, strong typography)

Examples: modern “landing page” oriented themes.

  • Strengths:
    • Great for first impressions and simple marketing-style home pages.
    • Clear hero area for one main message or call-to-action.
    • Usually good responsive behavior on mobile.
  • Weaknesses:
    • Less flexible for dense navigation or many utility links.
    • Event-heavy or content-heavy pages can feel crowded under a large banner.
    • Limited control of hero height and alignment without custom CSS.
  • Best fit for SBNC: Home page, “About SBNC,” and simple outreach pages.

3.2 Sidebar Navigation Themes

Layouts with a persistent left or right sidebar for navigation and quick links.

  • Strengths:
    • Good for content-heavy sites with many sections.
    • Navigation is always visible on desktop.
    • Works well for documentation-style pages or committee content.
  • Weaknesses:
    • Sidebar behavior on mobile can be inconsistent or feel cramped.
    • Less space for wide event images or large photo galleries.
    • Sometimes feels dated compared to full-width layouts.
  • Best fit for SBNC: Internal documentation, board/committee sections, FAQ-style content.

3.3 Simple Centered Layouts

Clean designs with centered content and standard top navigation.

  • Strengths:
    • Easy to scan and understand for non-technical users.
    • Typically stable across updates and theme versions.
    • Good for forms, event listings, and member-facing tools.
  • Weaknesses:
    • May feel generic if heavily used by other organizations.
    • Less room for “-magazine style” layouts or creative designs.
    • Requires stronger imagery and copy to feel distinctive.
  • Best fit for SBNC: Default for most member-facing pages and system pages.

4. Criteria for Choosing a Theme for SBNC

When SBNC selects a theme, we should evaluate it against:

  • Readability and accessibility
    • Clear contrast between text and background.
    • Comfortable font sizes for members using laptops and tablets.
    • Keyboard navigation and focus states that remain visible.
  • Navigation capacity
    • Can it handle the number of top-level pages we have without wrapping awkwardly?
    • Does it support simple secondary navigation where needed?
  • Event and photo usage
    • Do event listings look clean without heavy customization?
    • Is there enough room for occasional photo galleries or highlight images?
  • Responsiveness and browser behavior
    • Works reliably in current Safari, Chrome, Edge, and Firefox.
    • Adapts well to phone portrait, phone landscape, and tablets without per-device tweaks.
    • Behaves well for both mouse/trackpad and touch users.
  • Long-term stability
    • Good track record across WA updates.
    • Does not require fragile custom CSS for basic layouts.
