Design Thinking Workshop

November 18th at Ed & Jan's Home

Join us for a collaborative workshop where we'll review interview findings and design solutions together to improve 7-8 critical pages on the SBNC website.

üéØ Workshop Agenda

6:00-6:30 PM: The Reveal (30 min)

What we'll do: Share what the interviews uncovered! We'll present the patterns, pain points, and user stories from all the pre-work interviews.

What you'll learn: Which pages frustrate members, why, and what they're trying to accomplish.

6:30-7:00 PM: Prioritize (30 min)

What we'll do: As a group, agree on which 7-8 pages need fixing most urgently before term ends.

Your role: Help decide what matters most based on real user feedback and feasibility.

7:00-8:30 PM: Design Solutions (90 min)

What we'll do: Break into small groups. Each group sketches improvements for 2-3 pages using whiteboards, paper, or digital tools.

Your role: Brainstorm solutions, sketch ideas, think creatively about how to fix the problems we identified.

This is the fun part! No coding required - just ideas and creativity.

8:30-9:00 PM: Share & Plan (30 min)

What we'll do: Groups share their solutions. We agree on the plan and assign implementation tasks.

What you'll walk away with: Clear understanding of what gets fixed, how, and by when!

ü§î Frequently Asked Questions

What if I couldn't do pre-work interviews?

Come anyway! Pre-work is totally optional. Your input on solutions during the workshop is just as valuable. We'll catch you up on interview findings at the start.

Do I need to be technical or know about web design?

Not at all! We want diverse perspectives. If you use the SBNC website, you're qualified to suggest improvements. The tech team handles all the actual coding.

What should I bring?

Just bring yourself and your ideas! We'll provide all materials (paper, markers, whiteboards, etc.). Bring a laptop if you prefer digital sketching, but it's not required.

What is abbreviated Design Thinking?

We're using a streamlined version of Design Thinking focused on speed. Normally DT takes weeks or months. We're doing the essential parts (empathy interviews, prioritization, solution design, planning) in compressed time because we need to fix pages before term ends.
